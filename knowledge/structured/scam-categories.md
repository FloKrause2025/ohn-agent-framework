## What This KB Is For

This KB defines the 10 scam categories Researchy is authorised to shortlist. It also defines the relevance criteria (include/exclude rules), the audience profile OHN writes for, and the urgency scoring system for prioritising topics.

## Why the Agent Needs This

Without this KB, Researchy would shortlist any scam that appears on Reddit — including gaming scams, Discord NFT fraud, and other topics that are irrelevant to OHN's audience of adults aged 30–65. Every wasted shortlist entry burns owner review time and erodes confidence in Researchy's output.

> **If ignored:** Researchy shortlists irrelevant topics. Owners reject them with frustration. The feedback loop degrades. Content pipeline slows down.

---

## OHN Audience Profile

Every topic must be assessed against this exact audience profile before shortlisting.

| Dimension | Profile |
|---|---|
| **Primary demographic** | Adults aged 30–65 |
| **Primary persona** | Women aged 35–44 in USA, Canada, UK, Australia |
| **Relationship context** | Parents or grandparents of digital-native children |
| **Digital fluency** | Moderate — uses smartphones, WhatsApp, email, social media, online banking |
| **Motivation to share** | Protecting family members (parents, grandparents) from scams |
| **What they fear most** | Financial loss, identity theft, their parents being targeted |

> **The share test:** Would someone aged 35–44 share this video with their mum or dad after watching? If not — it does not belong in the shortlist.

## The 10 Approved Categories

### 🎣 Phishing
**What it covers:** Fake emails, fake login pages, credential harvesting, email impersonation of trusted brands.

**Include if:** The scam arrives via email, uses a cloned brand identity, or harvests login credentials through a fake page.

**Audience relevance:** HIGH — email is the primary communication channel for adults 30–65. Every banking, government, and retail interaction happens via email.

**Strong examples:** Gmail Dot Scam, fake HMRC/IRS tax email, fake bank security alert, fake Apple ID email.

---

### 📱 Smishing
**What it covers:** Scam SMS messages, fake text alerts, fake delivery notifications, fake bank texts.

**Include if:** The scam arrives via SMS and impersonates a trusted institution (bank, postal service, government, retail platform).

**Audience relevance:** HIGH — SMS is trusted more than email by older adults because phone numbers feel more personal. The same-thread spoofing vulnerability makes smishing particularly dangerous.

**Strong examples:** Fake Royal Mail / USPS delivery text, fake bank fraud alert SMS, fake HMRC tax refund text.

---

### 💻 Tech Support Scams
**What it covers:** Fake virus warnings, fake Microsoft/Apple support calls, remote access requests, fake device repair services.

**Include if:** The scam involves a fake authority figure (tech company, device manufacturer) claiming the victim's device is compromised, leading to a remote access request or payment.

**Audience relevance:** HIGH — older adults are disproportionately targeted and less likely to recognise that Microsoft/Apple do not call proactively. Financial losses are typically high (£500–£10,000+).

**Strong examples:** Fake Microsoft virus warning pop-up, fake Apple support call, fake BT/Virgin support engineer.

---

### 💸 Refund Scams
**What it covers:** Fake overpayment refunds, fake bank refund calls, fake subscription cancellation refunds, overpayment marketplace scams.

**Include if:** The scam involves a fabricated overpayment, refund, or subscription charge as the hook to extract payment or bank access.

**Audience relevance:** MEDIUM-HIGH — adults 30–65 regularly manage subscriptions, banking, and marketplace sales. The social engineering element (a helpful call from someone who "overpaid") works strongly on people who trust financial institutions.

**Strong examples:** Fake PayPal refund call, fake Amazon subscription refund, marketplace overpayment scam.

---

### 🪙 Crypto Scams
**What it covers:** Fake investment platforms, pig butchering (romance-to-investment pipeline), crypto giveaway fraud, fake celebrity endorsements.

**Include if:** The scam exploits the viewer's interest in investment returns or their relationship with someone who introduces them to a "platform."

**Audience relevance:** MEDIUM — crypto scams affect a wide age range but are particularly damaging for adults 40–65 who have accumulated savings. Pig butchering is especially relevant given its emotional manipulation component.

**Important:** Pure trading/technical crypto scams targeting young investors are lower priority. Focus on scams that could affect OHN's core demographic.

---

### 💕 Romance Scams
**What it covers:** Fake relationships built over time (weeks/months) to extract money, gift cards, or bank transfers.

**Include if:** The scam involves emotional manipulation through a fake relationship — typically on dating apps, social media, or WhatsApp.

**Audience relevance:** MEDIUM-HIGH — adults 40–65 are a primary target demographic for romance scams. Financial losses are among the highest of any scam category. Shame prevents reporting, making education particularly important.

---

### 🎭 Impersonation Scams
**What it covers:** Fake government officials (HMRC, IRS, DWP), fake police officers, fake bank fraud teams, fake family member emergency scams.

**Include if:** The scam involves impersonating a trusted authority figure or family member to create urgency and extract payment or access.

**Audience relevance:** HIGH — older adults are the primary target. The authority dynamic (fear of government/police) is powerful with this demographic. Grandparent scams (fake grandson/granddaughter in trouble) are extremely high-impact.

**Strong examples:** Fake HMRC arrest warrant call, fake bank fraud team calling to "move your money to safety", grandparent emergency scam.

---

### 📲 Social Media Scams
**What it covers:** Fake giveaways, hacked account impersonation, fake brand pages, fake sponsored ads leading to fraud sites.

**Include if:** The scam operates through social media platforms (Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp, TikTok) and targets users who use those platforms regularly.

**Audience relevance:** MEDIUM-HIGH — Facebook and Instagram are heavily used by adults 35–65. WhatsApp is used by virtually all demographics for family communication.

**Note:** Discord scams targeting gamers and young people are EXCLUDED — see Exclude list below.

---

### 📢 Fake Ad Scams
**What it covers:** Fake sponsored ads on Google/Meta leading to fraud sites, fake celebrity investment endorsements in paid ads, fake product/service ads.

**Include if:** The scam uses legitimate advertising infrastructure (Google Ads, Facebook/Instagram Ads) to promote a fraudulent product, service, or investment — particularly if it involves impersonating a known brand or public figure.

**Audience relevance:** HIGH — adults 35–65 are the primary target demographic for fake investment ads using celebrity endorsements. The legitimacy signals of a paid ad (appears on a trusted platform, looks professional) make this category particularly deceptive.

---

### 🤖 AI Scams
**What it covers:** Deepfake voice cloning, AI-generated video fraud, fake AI tools harvesting data, AI-generated phishing emails.

**Include if:** The scam explicitly uses AI-generated content (voice, video, text) as the deception mechanism — or exploits the viewer's curiosity about AI tools.

**Audience relevance:** GROWING — this category is the fastest-growing threat. AI voice cloning targeting grandparents ("mum, it's me — I need help") is extremely high-impact. Flag HIGH urgency when Researchy finds new AI scam variants.

## Relevance Filter — Include/Exclude

### INCLUDE a post only if ALL are true:
- ✅ The scam happens ONLINE (digital, internet-based)
- ✅ Relevant to adults aged 30–65 (they could realistically encounter this)
- ✅ Relevant to parents or grandparents (they could be targeted, or their family could warn them)
- ✅ NEW or TRENDING — not already covered by OHN (check Learning Memory)

### EXCLUDE a post if ANY are true:
- ❌ Physical / in-person fraud (door-to-door, street scams)
- ❌ Discord scams targeting gamers or young people
- ❌ Gaming scams (Roblox, Fortnite, Steam, any gaming platform)
- ❌ Vague post with no useful information
- ❌ Already covered by OHN with no meaningful new angle (per Learning Memory)

## Urgency Scoring

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 🔴 HIGH | 3+ Reddit posts before dedup (trending) AND/OR directly targets older adults AND/OR significant financial loss AND/OR brand new scam type AND/OR posted within 24 hours |
| 🟡 MEDIUM | 2 posts before dedup OR broad adult audience OR known type with new tactic OR posted within 48 hours |
| 🟢 LOW | Single post, limited audience relevance, older than 48 hours, worth monitoring but not urgent |

> **Tiebreaker rule:** Within the same urgency tier, rank by newest first. Break ties by duplicate post count (more posts = more trending).

## Shortlist Target

| Target | Rule |
|---|---|
| Aim for | 3–7 topics per run |
| Minimum | No minimum — if only 2 qualify, return 2 |
| Maximum | 7 — never pad the list to hit a number |

> **Quality over quantity.** A shortlist of 3 strong topics is better than 7 weak ones. Padding with weak topics wastes owner review time.
